Several things can trigger a nosebleed. The most common cause is dry air, either from having the heat on in winter or being in a hot, low-humidity climate. Other causes include: Nosebleeds usually aren’t serious. You can treat most by yourself at home by doing the following: Once the bleeding has stopped, do not touch or blow your nose. This may start it bleeding again. But if it does restart, gently blow your nose to get rid of anyblood clots. You can also spray adecongestantsuch asoxymetazoline(Afrin,Mucinex, or VicksSinex) in both nostrils. Then pinch your nostrils shut and breathe through yourmouthfor 5 to 10 minutes. Call your doctor if: If your doctor can’t get your nosebleed to stop with pressure, they might try: Cauterization.This procedure burns a blood vessel closed. After your doctor numbs your nose, they’ll use either a heated electronic device (an electrocautery) or a chemical called silver nitrate to close the leaky blood vessel. Packing.Your doctor puts a latex balloon or gauze into your nostril. This puts a lot of pressure on a blood vessel until it closes.
